Dan Wojtowicz

I am a junior Computer Science student at Illinois Institute of Technology, currently pursuing co-terminal Bachelor's and Master's degrees. In the field of Computer Science, I am interested in video game development and system architectures. Ever since I could remember, I have wondered how different computer systems were designed and interacted with each other, so some of my goals include understanding these systems to be able to improve upon them with my own ideas.

My first experience programming a computer occurred when I was about ten years old. At a family gathering, my cousin showed me some cool new things she had learned in her 8th grade Web Design class. It was then that I wrote my first HTML page using the standards of that era. Our lessons stopped there, unfortunately, and for many years I was writing pages strictly with HTML and no other technologies like CSS, JavaScript, or back-end languages. My sites were ugly, but they did the job of displaying content. Since then I have learned a lot more about web design, but through this class I hope to improve my skills and standardize my web design style.

A hobby of mine is organizing and competing in electronic sports like League of Legends. In high school I created a gaming club focused around Super Smash Bros. Melee, a simple but very technically-deep game. The club grew quickly, as did our events. I used my abilities in web design to create a website for the club and wrote applications that streamlined club processes. In addition to these things, I created marketing materials and graphics for each tournament live stream. The work so far has culminated into a massive event we held in the summer of 2017, where I combined my experience of TOing (tournament organizing) with programming to create an event that imitated professional gaming tournaments like EVO.

In my free time, I do freelance work, usually building websites or building and fixing computers. During the summer of 2017 I had an internship position at Carl Buddig & Company; I spent my time there updating their existing code to the latest .NET version and developing new features in their internal knowledge base. At that job I learned to love high level languages like Visual Basic and all-you-can-eat development environments like Visual Studio.

In the programming world, I see myself as a jack of many trades. If there is a technology I'm interested in, I at least want to understand the basics. One area where I am invested in learning the deep-level internals, however, is game development. So far I have been using frameworks like LibGDX or manipulating the DOM to create games, but I would like to dig deeper and create my own game engine. I mainly am interested in creating competitive games, such as the ones I play and create tournaments for. I look forward to a career in a game development studio or even my own gaming company.
